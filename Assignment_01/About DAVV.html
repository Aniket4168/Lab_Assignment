Devi Ahilya Vishwavidyalaya (DAVV)

DAVV – established in 1964 in the heart of Indore – is a prestigious university with a rich legacy. Originally known as the University of Indore, it was renamed to honor the visionary ruler, Devi Ahilya Bai Holkar.

With over 153 affiliated colleges and a diverse range of programs from Science and Engineering to Law and Journalism, DAVV stands as a pillar of academic excellence in Central India.

DAVV’s expansive network, encompassing three major campuses – Nalanda, Takshashila, and Avanti – provides an environment where research, innovation, and cultural enrichment flourish.
